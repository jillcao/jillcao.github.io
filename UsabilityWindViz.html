A usability study of wind visualization techniques

Tags: usability testing, statistical experiment, quantative analysis, usability of visualizations

Time frame: 2007

Lack of empirical user studies has been identified as one of the major problems within the field of scientific visualization. This thesis aims to address this problem by contributing empirical evidence of the effectiveness of several commonly used visualization methods for 2-dimensional wind data gathered through a lab usability experiment. Statistical analyses of this data revealed the best visualization methods for displaying wind data.
